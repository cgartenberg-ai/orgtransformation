> **Definition:** Companies implementing lab-like organizational models to commercialize AI internally WITHOUT formal branding, lab naming, or public announcement. ### Key Patterns Identified

**Leader-Lab-Crowd Implementations (Not Using That Terminology):**
- **P&G**: ChatPG = "Crowd" tool; AI Factory = "Lab"; brand managers = "Leaders"
- **Colgate-Palmolive**: AI Hub = central "Lab"; 4,000 users = "Crowd"; CAIO = "Leader"
- **Clorox**: Digital Core = "Lab"; 90-day cycles = rapid commercialization
- **Allstate**: AI Factory = explicit Lab function; cross-functional = "Crowd" integration
- **Citi**: AI Champions = explicit "Leader" role; Stylus = "Crowd" tool
- **Disney**: OTE = "Lab" function; park-wide AI = "Crowd" deployment

**"Secret Cyborgs" Indicators:**
- Bank of America's 95% AI usage suggests massive unofficial adoption preceded formal programs
- General Mills' 20,000 MillsChat users indicates bottom-up pressure formalized
- P&G's 30,000 ChatPG users grew organically before official scaling

**Internal → External Product Path:**
- Walmart (Route Optimization → GoLocal)
- Kroger (84.51° retail analytics → external CPG platform)
- UnitedHealth (internal AI → external marketplace)
- This is the hallmark of a Product/Venture Lab even without the name

### Methodology Note on Unnamed/Informal Section

The "Unnamed/Informal AI Product Teams" section was created to address a gap in traditional AI org model research: the focus on formally named labs misses the "quiet transformation" happening at companies like Clorox, Bank of America, 3M, and Walmart that are:

1. **Implementing lab-like structures** without calling them "labs"
2. **Following Leader-Lab-Crowd patterns** without using that terminology
3. **Commercializing internal AI externally** (classic Product Lab pattern)
4. **Discovering "secret cyborg" usage** and formalizing it into programs

These represent potentially the **majority of enterprise AI transformation** but receive less coverage because they lack the marketing appeal of "Google X" or "Anthropic Labs."
