# Purpose Claims: Analytical Notes

Accumulates cross-cutting observations from purpose claims scanning sessions. Updated after each session. These notes feed directly into the purpose × AI transformation paper.

---

## Emerging Patterns

### 1. Purpose Claims Escalate With Structural Disruption
Purpose claims intensify at moments of structural change — the MSL founding (utopian), the 600-person layoff (transformation + sacrifice), the Q4 earnings call (employee-deal + CapEx authorization). This is consistent with the hypothesis that purpose claims do organizational work: they're authorization mechanisms, not decoration.

**Evidence:** meta-ai (11 claims concentrated around 3 structural events)

### 2. Two Speakers, Two Registers
At Meta, Zuckerberg speaks in utopian and identity registers ("new era for humanity," "talent-dense," "personal superintelligence for everyone") while Wang speaks in transformation and sacrifice registers ("fewer conversations," "disruptive but necessary"). The division of rhetorical labor is clean — founder handles vision, CAIO handles operational justification.

**Evidence:** meta-ai (Zuckerberg 9 claims, Wang 2 claims, no overlap in claim type)

**Open question:** Does this founder/operator split hold at other specimens? Nadella vs. Jassy? Lutke as a case where one person plays both roles?

### 3. "Talent-Dense" as Recurring Identity Frame
"Talent-dense" appears in at least 3 separate Zuckerberg statements across 7 months (Threads post, MSL founding context, Q4 earnings). It's not a one-off — it's become Meta's core organizational identity claim for MSL. The organizational identity is defined by who's in the room, not what they produce.

**Evidence:** meta-ai--002, meta-ai--010 (plus MSL founding memo context)

**Theoretical connection:** Relates to Gibbons & Henderson on relational contracts — identity claims may function as coordination devices that substitute for formal incentive structures.

### 4. CapEx Authorization via Purpose
$115-135B CapEx has no standard NPV justification. Zuckerberg's authorization mechanism is purpose: "beginning of a new era," "personal superintelligence for everyone," "we have the resources." The financial case ("profitable over some period") is deliberately vague. This is the clearest case in our sample of purpose substituting for profit signals at massive scale.

**Evidence:** meta-ai--007, meta-ai--009, meta-ai--011

**Theoretical connection:** Gartenberg & Zenger on purpose enabling long-term investment. But Meta pushes this further — it's not just long-term, it's uncertain-horizon.

### 5. Competitive Positioning via Purpose
Meta.com/superintelligence explicitly contrasts Meta's "personal empowerment" vision with unnamed competitors who "believe superintelligence should be directed centrally towards automating all valuable work." Purpose claims are doing market differentiation work — defining what Meta is by defining what it's not.

**Evidence:** meta-ai--008

**Open question:** Do other specimens use purpose to differentiate competitively? Eli Lilly ("breakthrough therapeutics") vs. generic pharma? Anthropic ("responsible AI") vs. other labs?

**UPDATE (Batch 1):** YES. Anthropic is the clearest case: "having at least one player that had a strong compass" explicitly positions Anthropic against other AI labs. Amodei's founding narrative is entirely competitive-purpose: we exist BECAUSE the others lack a moral compass. Eli Lilly's "We have a higher calling" also differentiates from competitors who view AI as a threat to their business model.

**SEARCH QUERY ADDED (from Batch 2 onward):** A counter-positioning search query was added to the standard query list in SKILL.md — one of eight queries that all run equally for every specimen. This is NOT a prioritized or weighted dimension. The same protocol applies as for all claim types: record what you find, don't hunt for confirmation. "None found" for counter-positioning is data, just as "none found" for any claim type is data. The theoretical interest is real but must not create motivated search — we are botanists recording what grows, not gardeners cultivating what we want to see.

### 6. Identity Claims Cluster in Transcript Sources
12 of 17 identity claims (71%) came from podcast transcripts, not press or earnings calls. Leaders reveal organizational identity in conversational contexts where they speak freely — not in formal statements crafted for investors. This has methodological implications: web-search-only scanning systematically underrepresents identity claims.

**Evidence:** Eli Lilly went from 0 claims (web search) to 7 claims (transcript scan). Microsoft gained 8 additional claims from transcript scan. Anthropic's 14 claims came entirely from one transcript.

**Implication:** The purpose claims registry must prioritize transcript sources. Updated protocol accordingly.

### 7. Anti-Scale as Organizational Identity
Both Ricks (Eli Lilly) and Amodei (Anthropic) define their organizations AGAINST scale. Ricks: "Scale's bad... 300 people with a focused mission." Amodei: "seven people who really carry the values." This is identity-through-constraint — purpose is maintained by staying small enough that values propagate through direct contact rather than formal systems.

**Evidence:** eli-lilly--001, anthropic--013

**Theoretical connection:** Relates to Dunbar numbers and the limits of relational contracts (Gibbons & Henderson). Purpose claims may function as a coordination mechanism that breaks down at scale — which is why these leaders protect small-scale structures.

### 8. "North Star" Is Not Convergent Language
Searched all 6 specimens for "north star" in AI context. Only Zuckerberg uses it (meta-ai--005). Zero instances across Nadella, Lutke, McDermott, Ricks, Jassy, or Amodei. This suggests "north star" is Zuckerberg's personal rhetorical device, not a common CEO framing for AI transformation.

**Evidence:** Null results across 6 supplemental searches

### 9. Shopify: Pure Employee-Deal
4 of 5 Shopify claims are employee-deal. Lutke's approach to AI transformation is almost entirely about resetting the employment contract — no utopian framing, no sacrifice-justification, no direction-under-uncertainty. This makes Shopify a clean archetype: AI as new baseline expectation, full stop. The organizational work is contractual, not visionary.

**Evidence:** shopify--001 through shopify--005

## Emerging Hypotheses (to test as we scan more specimens)

### H1: Claim type distribution varies systematically with structural model
- M4 Hub-and-Spoke (meta-ai): heavy on utopian + identity — need to justify coordination overhead
- M3 Embedded/Contextual (shopify): almost entirely employee-deal — Lutke resets the employment contract, no utopian framing at all
- M4/Structural (microsoft, eli-lilly): identity-heavy — organizational self-definition as authorization mechanism
- M9 AI-Native (anthropic): richest of all — 14 claims spanning all 6 types. The organizational identity IS the purpose.
- **Early signal supports H1.** Model type shapes claim type. Need more specimens.

### H2: Founder-led firms produce more utopian claims than professionally managed firms
- Meta (founder Zuckerberg): 3 utopian claims — SUPPORTS
- Shopify (founder Lutke): 0 utopian claims — CONTRADICTS
- Anthropic (founder Amodei): 1 utopian claim, but 5 identity — MIXED (identity ≠ utopian)
- Amazon (founder Jassy): 0 utopian claims — CONTRADICTS
- Microsoft (hired CEO Nadella): 0 utopian claims — consistent with hypothesis direction
- **H2 is too simple.** Founder status enables but doesn't determine utopian framing. Zuckerberg is the outlier, not the pattern. Revised hypothesis: founder authority determines the RANGE of claim types available, not a specific type.

### H3: Purpose claims are concentrated in the first year of structural transformation
- Meta's claims cluster around MSL founding (Jun 2025) and first major restructuring (Oct 2025) — SUPPORTS
- Cannot fully test yet — need temporal data from more specimens
- Eli Lilly is a counter-case: Ricks's claims reference a 15+ year structural identity, not a recent transformation

### H4: "None" specimens tell us something about technocratic vs. purpose-driven transformation
- Eli Lilly initially appeared to be "none" but this was a **methodology artifact** — web search found 0, transcript scan found 7
- ServiceNow at 2 claims may be genuinely low-density, but McDermott is known for big statements — the low count may reflect limited source access
- **H4 cannot be tested reliably until the transcript gap is closed.** Any "none" result from web search alone is suspect.

## Claim Type Observations

### Utopian claims do the heaviest lifting
At Meta, utopian claims authorize the largest commitments ($14.3B acquisition, $115-135B CapEx, "new era for humanity"). They convert financial decisions into civilizational bets. This is the most powerful purpose claim type — and potentially the most brittle if the vision doesn't materialize.

### Employee-deal claims are the most structurally consequential
"Single very talented person" and "flattening teams" directly predict headcount changes. These aren't aspirational — they're operational directives wrapped in purpose language. Employee-deal claims are where purpose meets the org chart.

### Direction-under-uncertainty claims are the most theoretically interesting
"Profitable over some period" is purpose substituting for a discounted cash flow model. This is the Gartenberg & Zenger mechanism in action: when profit signals are weak, purpose provides direction. The hedging in these claims is analytically valuable — it reveals the actual uncertainty the leader is managing.

### 15. Patient-Outcome Anchoring Is Universal in Pharma
Every pharma specimen connects AI investment to patient outcomes. This is the teleological backbone: AI exists to serve patients. Even transformation-framing and direction-under-uncertainty claims circle back to patient benefit. Contrasts with tech companies where teleological anchors are more diffuse ("new era for humanity," productivity gains).

**Evidence:** All 3 pharma specimens — pfizer (10/10 reference patients), novo-nordisk (8/11), roche-genentech (14/18)

### 16. The CEO Gap: Pharma AI Claims Come From AI/Digital Leaders
Novo Nordisk CEO yielded ZERO AI-context purpose claims. Roche-Genentech's 18 claims are overwhelmingly from Regev (EVP, not CEO Schinecker). In pharma, AI purpose claims come from the AI/digital leadership layer, not the CEO. Tech companies are the opposite (Zuckerberg, Nadella, Lutke = CEO-centric).

**Evidence:** novo-nordisk (6 speakers, 0 CEO), roche-genentech (16/18 from Regev, 2 from Schinecker)

**Theoretical connection:** May reflect pharma governance — CEOs address financial audiences while AI leaders address technical audiences. The purpose claim function is delegated because AI expertise is delegated.

### 17. Regev as Outlier Data Point
Aviv Regev (roche-genentech) produced 18 claims spanning all 7 types — possibly the richest single speaker in our collection. Compare: Zuckerberg 9, Amodei 14, Nadella ~8. Her rhetorical range is extraordinary: utopian ("human history"), sacrifice ("never going to be a drug"), employee-deal ("accent" metaphor), direction-under-uncertainty ("no Plan B").

**Evidence:** roche-genentech--001 through --016

### 18. Direction-Under-Uncertainty Overrepresented in Pharma
Pharma specimens produce more direction-under-uncertainty claims proportionally than tech. Consistent with pharma's long time horizons and genuine uncertainty about AI's drug discovery impact. Leaders can say "we are still learning" (Iversen) because drug discovery inherently operates under deep uncertainty.

**Evidence:** novo-nordisk (4/11 = 36%), roche-genentech (3/18)

### 10. Purpose Claims Are Context-Dependent Performance (Benioff Contradiction)
Benioff says "I think AI augments people, but I don't know if it necessarily replaces them" in a Fortune interview (July 2025), then says "I've reduced it from 9,000 heads to about 5,000, because I need less heads" on a podcast (September 2025). The same CEO produces augmentation claims in formal press interviews and replacement claims in conversational podcast settings. This is evidence that purpose claims are context-dependent rhetorical performance, not stable beliefs.

**Evidence:** salesforce--005 vs. salesforce--007; also salesforce--004 (utopian 'last generation of CEOs') vs. salesforce--009 ('this is reality, at least for me')

**Theoretical connection:** Connects to impression management literature but with a twist — the register shift isn't audience-dependent (both are public), it's format-dependent (formal interview vs. conversational podcast). Podcast contexts elicit more blunt, operationally honest statements.

### 11. The Reversal Case: Purpose Claims Can Fail (Klarna)
Klarna is the only specimen in our collection where a CEO publicly reversed a purpose claim. The arc: "doing the equivalent work of 700 full-time agents" (Feb 2024) → "cost unfortunately seems to have been a too predominant evaluation factor... what you end up having is lower quality" (May-June 2025). Siemiatkowski acknowledged the sacrifice was NOT justified by the purpose.

**Evidence:** klarna--006 (pre-reversal) vs. klarna--001 (post-reversal). Also klarna--004 ('my tech bros are being slightly not to the point') — explicitly calling out other CEOs' framing.

**Theoretical connection:** Reversal cases are rare because admitting purpose claims failed is reputationally costly. Klarna was able to reverse because: (1) the IPO succeeded regardless ($19.6B valuation), (2) the 'quality' frame converts failure into learning, (3) Siemiatkowski reframes the reversal as forward-looking strategy ('way of the future for us'). For our paper, this is the strongest counter-evidence to the utopian framing pattern.

### 12. Anti-Utopian Register: Sweet and Siemiatkowski
Two CEOs in Batch 2 break from the utopian consensus: Sweet ("value realization has been underwhelming for many") and Siemiatkowski ("my tech bros are being slightly not to the point"). Both ground their anti-utopian claims in operational experience — Sweet has seen client AI projects fail to scale, Siemiatkowski tried aggressive AI substitution and watched quality degrade. This creates a natural contrast group with the utopian speakers (Benioff, Zuckerberg).

**Evidence:** accenture-openai--002, accenture-openai--007, klarna--001, klarna--004

**Open question:** Does anti-utopian register correlate with having experienced AI failure? Sweet has seen client failures; Siemiatkowski experienced his own. Benioff and Zuckerberg have not (yet) had comparable public failures.

### 13. "North Star" Update
Pattern #8 needs revision. Sweet IS using "North Star" — making her the second CEO (after Zuckerberg) to use this exact phrase. But unlike Zuckerberg's product-oriented North Star, Sweet's is character-oriented (excellence, confidence, humility). The phrase is more common than initially thought but deployed for different purposes: Zuckerberg uses it for strategic direction, Sweet uses it for personal leadership philosophy.

**Evidence:** accenture-openai--005

### 14. CEO Succession Changes Rhetorical Register (SK Telecom)
Ryu Young-sang launched the AI CIC with utopian "golden era" framing; his successor Jung Jai-hun pivoted to efficiency and humility ("Chief Change Officer," "We cannot create real change by simply working harder"). Two CEOs, two registers, one transformation. The predecessor provides the vision; the successor provides the operational discipline. Unlike the Zuckerberg/Wang division (simultaneous, across roles), this is temporal (sequential, across leadership transitions).

**Evidence:** sk-telecom--003 (Ryu, utopian) vs. sk-telecom--005 (Jung, direction-under-uncertainty)

## Updated Hypotheses

### H1 UPDATE: Claim type distribution varies with structural model — MODERATED BY INDUSTRY

- **H1 REVISION (Batch 4):** Structural model alone doesn't predict claim type. Industry context is a moderator. Same M4 structure produces different claim profiles depending on time horizons, regulatory environment, and CEO vs. AI-leader purpose delegation.

- Benioff on podcast: "I need less heads" / "This is reality"

- Sweet in Fortune interview: "it was hard" / "value realization underwhelming"
- **H5 needs testing:** Do ALL CEOs shift register across contexts? Or is this specific to Benioff?
