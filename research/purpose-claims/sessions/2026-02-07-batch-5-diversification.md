Batch 5 was designed for industry diversification. Batches 1-4 covered tech founders, tech incumbents, and pharma. This batch opens three entirely new institutional contexts:

- **Financial Services** (UBS) — regulated, relationship-driven, governance-heavy

## Analytical Notes

### New Patterns

**#19: Institutional context shapes purpose rhetoric radically.** Defense (existential threat framing), logistics (efficiency/survival), financial services (trust/governance), and tech (civilizational vision) produce qualitatively different purpose claim patterns even when the underlying structural model is similar. This is the strongest evidence yet for H1 (industry moderates model-claim relationship).

**#20: Shrinkage-as-transformation is a distinct rhetorical move.** UPS's "better not bigger" framing and Pentagon's "dissolve three oversight bodies" are both claiming that subtraction IS the transformation, not a side effect. This hasn't appeared in tech or pharma specimens where transformation = growth.

**#21: Public mission vs. commercial mission creates different purpose dynamics.** Pentagon claims invoke duty, national security, and taxpayer accountability — authorization comes from external mandate rather than shareholder returns. This is structurally different from all private-sector specimens.

### Updated Hypotheses

- **H1 (industry moderates model-claim)**: STRONGLY supported. Four industries now represented with clearly distinct rhetorical patterns.
- **H5 (new)**: Purpose claims in operations-heavy firms (UPS, logistics) anchor transformation in efficiency/survival rather than vision/mission. The "why" is concrete and measurable, not aspirational.
