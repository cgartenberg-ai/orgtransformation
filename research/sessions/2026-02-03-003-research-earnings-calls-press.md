## Broader Trends

### 1. The CapEx Arms Race Reaches Breaking Point
Combined 2026 AI CapEx commitments from just four companies: Meta ($115-135B) + Amazon ($150B+) + Microsoft ($150B projected annualized) + Google ($91-93B) = **$500-530B** in a single year. The market is starting to question ROI — Microsoft's $375B market cap wipe on Jan 29 is the first major correction. The "AI Efficiency" narrative pivot signals a new phase: from "spend to build" to "show me the returns."

### 2. Zuckerberg's "Single Talented Person" = Management Delayering in Action
Zuckerberg's Q4 statement that "what many big teams do can now be accomplished by a single, very talented person" is the most explicit CEO statement we've seen on AI-driven org flattening. This + Amazon's "reducing layers" language + prior Microsoft/Nadella delayering statements = convergence across Big Tech on management delayering as structural consequence of AI.

### 3. Salesforce as the Cleanest AI Restructuring Case Study
Salesforce is the best case study we have of a company simultaneously (a) building AI products (Agentforce), (b) using those products to restructure its own workforce (4,000 support jobs → redeployment), and (c) selling the restructuring capability to others (AgentExchange, "$6T digital labor market"). The numbers are specific and public: 84% resolution rate, 2% escalation, $500M ARR. This is our densest evidence of genuine AI-driven structural change (not AI-washing).

### 4. The Jassy Contradiction
Jassy says Amazon's 30K cuts are "culture, not AI" — but separately says AI will reduce corporate headcount, and internal reports confirm AI agents replacing tasks. This is the AI-washing signal playing out in real time: CEOs hedging between "we're restructuring because of culture/efficiency" and "AI is changing everything." The truth is probably both.

### 5. Earnings Calls as Primary Source for AI Org Structure
This session confirms that quarterly earnings calls are the highest-density source for structural AI observations. On-the-record CEO statements about flattening, delayering, agent deployment, and CapEx are more reliable than press coverage. Should elevate earnings-calls to Tier 1 source.
