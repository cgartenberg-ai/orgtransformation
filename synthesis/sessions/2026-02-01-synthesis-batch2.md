## Taxonomy Proposals
| Proposal | Type | Confidence |
|----------|------|------------|
| Model 2 sub-types: client-facing CoE, internal operations CoE, domain-specific CoE | Sub-type addition | Medium — NTT DATA's triple-CoE is the clearest example. Parallels existing Model 5 and Model 6 sub-types. |

NTT DATA's triple-CoE structure (OpenAI CoE for clients, CIO-office CoE for internal, Virtual CoE for CX) provides the strongest evidence yet that Model 2 is fragmenting. The taxonomy currently treats "Center of Excellence" as monolithic, but the evidence suggests at least three functional variants:
- **2a: Client-facing CoE** — builds AI services for external clients (NTT DATA OpenAI CoE)
- **2b: Internal operations CoE** — manages enterprise AI integration (NTT DATA CIO office)
- **2c: Domain-specific CoE** — focused on a single business vertical (NTT DATA Virtual CoE for CX)

This mirrors how Model 5 has 5a/5b/5c and Model 6 has 6a/6b/6c. Other existing specimens that might map to these sub-types: Infosys (2a+2b), LG Electronics (2b), US Cyber Command (2b), Publicis (2a).

## Key Insights for Executives
- **Management delayering is emerging as a structural consequence of AI**, not just headcount reduction. Both UPS and Microsoft are explicitly targeting management layers because AI reduces coordination complexity between units. Leaders should expect flatter org structures as AI matures — plan for it rather than resist it.
- **The CAIO pipeline is diversifying rapidly.** Internal tech leaders (CTO→CAIO), enterprise IT veterans (CIO→CAIO), and consumer tech product builders are all viable sources. The right background depends on whether the organization needs governance (enterprise IT), product innovation (consumer tech), or domain transformation (internal promotion).
- **"Portfolio of accountable owners" may be the next evolution of centralized AI leadership.** PwC's framework suggests the CAIO role is naturally evolving from single executive to distributed governance — a pattern that may be more sustainable than expecting one person to coordinate all AI activity.

## Key Insights for Academics
- **Convergent management delayering across industries** (logistics + tech) suggests AI's structural impact goes beyond task automation to organizational architecture. This connects to research on coordination costs (Thompson, 1967) and information processing (Galbraith, 1973) — AI may be reducing the information processing load that justified middle management.
- **Mechanism #9 is broadening beyond its original definition.** The evidence now shows the discriminator is product/technology-shipping experience, not specifically consumer tech. This suggests the underlying mechanism is about operational mindset transfer, not industry-specific experience.
- **Model 2 (CoE) fragmentation into sub-types** parallels how other organizational forms specialize under environmental pressure. NTT DATA's triple-CoE is a response to the dual explore/execute tension unique to IT services firms that must transform internally AND deliver AI externally.
