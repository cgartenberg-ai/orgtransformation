# Classification Quick Reference

## Structural Models at a Glance

| Model | One-Line Test | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **1. Research Lab** | Publishes papers, 3-10 year horizon | DeepMind, FAIR |
| **2. Center of Excellence** | Enables others, doesn't build products | Many bank AI CoEs |
| **3. Embedded Teams** | AI built into product teams, no central | Product-specific AI |
| **4. Hybrid/Hub-and-Spoke** | Central + distributed execution | Many enterprises |
| **5. Product/Venture Lab** | Builds products/companies from AI | Google X, Anthropic |
| **6. Unnamed/Informal** | No formal structure, quiet adoption | P&G ChatPG, BofA |
| **7. Tiger Teams** | Time-boxed sprints, people return | Samsung C-Lab |

## Model 5 Sub-Types

| Sub-Type | Key Indicator | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **5a. Internal Incubator** | Products stay in parent company | Adobe Firefly |
| **5b. Venture Builder** | Spin-offs become independent | Google X → Waymo |
| **5c. Platform-to-Product** | Internal tool sold externally | Walmart GoLocal |

## Model 6 Sub-Types

| Sub-Type | Key Indicator | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **6a. Enterprise-Wide** | 80%+ adoption, top-down mandate | BofA 95% AI usage |
| **6b. Centralized-but-Unnamed** | Central team, no lab branding | P&G ChatPG |
| **6c. Grassroots** | Adoption preceded formal structure | "Secret cyborg" patterns |

## Ambidexterity Orientations

| Orientation | One-Line Test |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Structural** | Separate units for explore vs. execute |
| **Contextual** | Same people do both, switch as needed |
| **Temporal** | Org cycles between explore and execute phases |

## 10 Mechanisms

1. **Protect Off-Strategy Work** — CEO shields deviations from middle management
2. **Bonus Teams That Kill Projects** — Reward fast failure
3. **Embed Product at Research Frontier** — Product teams work with researchers
4. **Consumer-Grade UX for Employee Tools** — Erica → Erica for Employees
5. **Deploy to Thousands Before You Know What Works** — Skip pilots, go wide
6. **Merge Competing AI Teams Under Single Leader** — Consolidate for coordination
7. **Put Executives on the Tools** — 8+ hours/week hands-on
8. **Log Everything When Regulators Watch** — Audit trails as advantage
9. **Hire CAIOs from Consumer Tech** — Product people, not IT
10. **Productize Internal Advantages** — Internal tools become revenue
